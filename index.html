Current Litigation Status: This case (Patterson v. United States Postal Service, Case No. 2:25-cv-05145) is currently pending before the U.S. District Court for the Central District of California.

The Story

My name is Jeff Patterson. For nine years, I served as a City Carrier Technician with the United States Postal Service. I am currently proceeding pro se (without an attorney) in a federal civil rights lawsuit asserting claims under the Rehabilitation Act and the Age Discrimination in Employment Act.

Why This Matters

This case raises broader questions about how federal agencies treat disabled workers and whether written medical restrictions are honored in practice. Standing up now helps ensure that others are not placed in the same position of choosing between their health and their livelihood.

The Fight

After sustaining a documented spinal injury in the line of duty, I entered into a written modified-duty agreement (PS Form 2499) that strictly limited my work hours to protect my health. My lawsuit alleges that management repeatedly violated these restrictions, forcing me to work beyond my physical limits, which ultimately led to adverse employment actions and the loss of my career.

I have filed a First Amended Complaint, responded to dispositive motions, and am actively participating in discovery. I am fighting not just for my own pension, but to ensure that disabled workers are treated with the dignity federal law requires.

The Personal Cost

Since my termination, I have lost my income and health insurance. I live with a chronic medical condition requiring ongoing treatment. The loss of financial stability has created a housing crisis during the pendency of this litigation.

"I am also navigating this case while grieving the recent loss of my wife, Toni. Continuing this fight is both a matter of survival and a way of honoring her memory. She believed in standing up for what is right, no matter the odds."
